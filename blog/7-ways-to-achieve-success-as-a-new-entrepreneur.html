7 Ways to Achieve Success as a New Entrepreneur

Mon Jun 10 2019

FOCUS ON ONE CONCEPT

I can’t stress this enough. Even if it sounds appealing, avoid jumping into every business opportunity that comes your way. Doing so will just cause you to spread yourself thin with too many ideas and ventures and you’ll end up exhausted and unmotivated. Make the goal to just focus on the one concept that you love and are passionate about. You’ll be more likely to achieve success with that one idea instead of ten.

ACT LIKE A START-UP

Let’s face it, when you’re just starting out you won’t have the money to spend on nice offices, fancy computers or any other extra things. That’s okay! Some of the most successful companies in the world started out in basements or tiny offices. Practice being frugal for the next few weeks, months or even years until your business really takes off. I promise your financial diligence will pay off in the end.

KNOW WHEN IT’S TIME TO WALK AWAY 

As hard as it is to accept, not all of your ideas are going to work out. Know when it’s time to walk away and leave that idea behind. If you are sinking tons of time and money into an idea that is going no where, it might be best to start pursuing something else. Determine what you could have done differently and how you can learn from your mistakes. Don’t be afraid of failure, the lessons you learn from your mistakes could very well lead to your success.

KEEP A JOURNAL OF IDEAS AND THINGS THAT YOU LEARN 

If a great idea comes to mind, write it down in a journal or your phone. You don’t want your inspiration to disappear because you didn’t write it down. The same goes for what you learn concerning entrepreneurship, starting your business, etc. Write down everything, as it will give you extra reassurance when things get tough.

TAKE TIME TO DISCONNECT

It’s all too easy to become completely consumed with your work as an entrepreneur, but disconnecting from your work is important for your body and mind to function at their best. Putting the work away an hour or two before bed each evening will help you feel more refreshed, rejuvenated and ready to go the next morning.

DEVELOP AN ELEVATOR PITCH 

Know your business inside and out – your mission, goals, and why people should be interested in your product or service. Develop a pitch that is 30 seconds or less that you can have ready to inform potential customers and investors with why they should be interested in your product or service. Your elevator pitch can truly make or break your start-up, so I certainly recommend spending some time on developing it!

JUST DO IT 

If anything, this is one of the most important things new entrepreneurs should know. Even if you are hesitant about getting your idea out there or starting your business, there will never be a perfect time to get started, so start now! Quit worrying about what “might” or “might not” happen and just do it. You never know where your idea can take you!

In all honesty, the world of entrepreneurship will require a lot from you, but I can guarantee that these tips will help you feel more comfortable as a first-timer. Before you know it, you’ll be on your way to becoming a successful entrepreneur!
