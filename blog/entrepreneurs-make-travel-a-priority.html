Why Every Entrepreneur Should Make Travel a Priority

Fri Jan 19 2018

Many people tend to think of travel as an unnecessary activity. One that just uses up valuable time and money, and is better suited for retirees and fresh college grads. But, the fact of the matter is, travel plays an important role in becoming a more successful entrepreneur. Travel teaches valuable lessons and strengthens life skills that can directly impact your entrepreneurial ventures. In fact, 94% of American business leaders believe that travel gives them a competitive advantage in their industry.

Still not convinced to make travel a priority in your life? These four reasons may very well change your mind about the way you view travel.

1. It shifts your perspective

Most entrepreneurs tend to get into the habit of running their business the same way, day in and day out. They utilize the same resources and assets, because that is what has worked for them. This is where travel plays a large role at helping you realize how limiting such a mind-set really is.

Travel is a great way to shift your perspective and your way of thinking. Experiencing new cultures and new environments will help you understand that your way of thinking isn’t the only one, or even the best one out there. If you really want your business to grow and expand, you must be more willing to try and adopt new ideas. Adopting a new perspective will allow you to work and communicate more effectively with your team, investors and clients.

2. It can boost your creativity

It can be so easy to put all of your time and energy into your business, but if you don’t make time to get away from your desk it can be really hard to keep the creativity flowing. Usually when you’re faced with a creative block, it’s wise to go for a walk or take a nap. So, if stepping away from the desk for just a few minutes can help boost creativity, wouldn’t traveling increase your creative thinking even more? Traveling can help spark new ideas and make you feel more inspired. Even just the simple act of thinking about traveling can be a huge creativity boost.

3. It will prevent burnout

Nothing is worse than feeling burned out from over-working. This is one of the reasons taking time to travel is so important. Travel is one of the best ways to relieve stress, as it allows you to take a break from the daily grind. Leaving on a trip, even just for a couple days, will help you to relax and disconnect. Once you return, you’ll be ready to hit the ground running.

4. It will teach you how to adapt

Entrepreneurship requires you to learn to expect the unexpected, and the same goes with traveling. Traveling teaches you how to adapt to different situations. You’ll be living out of a suitcase for several days, you’ll most likely experience delayed flights, you’ll need to learn how to communicate with those who may speak a different language, and you very well could lose a valuable or two along the way. Adapting to such situations is going to require you to adopt resilience, flexibility and the willingness to accept the uncertain. These are all traits that can help you as an entrepreneur. Consider travel as a way to practice testing your limits and to learn you how to face trials – crucial skills for business owners to possess.

Traveling, whether you do so for business or pleasure, is guaranteed to make you a more confident and successful entrepreneur and leader.

Where will you travel to next?
